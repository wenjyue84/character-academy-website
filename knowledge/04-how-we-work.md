# 04 — How We Work

## The flow

> **Needs → Standards → Programme → Implementation → Certification → Sustainability**

Every engagement follows the same six-step backbone. The sequence matters — each step depends on the one before it, and skipping a step is how you end up with training that doesn't fit the job.

## The six steps

### 1. Industry & Organisational Needs Analysis
We start on the ground. What does the industry actually demand? What's broken inside this specific organisation — a capability gap, a retention problem, an inconsistent training system? The goal is to align what we build next with real operational needs, not with generic training brochures.

### 2. Standards Alignment
We map those needs to the **National Occupational Skills Standard (NOSS)** and the national certification framework (SKM / DKM / DLKM). This is what turns a custom training plan into a nationally recognised one.

### 3. Programme Design
We design competency-based training pathways — modular, level-appropriate, and assessable — so that every learner can demonstrate what they can do, not just what they've heard.

### 4. Implementation
We deliver. Classroom learning is integrated with workplace practice (and, where appropriate, with the **ADI-Perantisan** apprenticeship model where 70–80% of the training happens at the employer's site).

### 5. Certification
We facilitate the formal competency assessment. Successful learners leave with an SKM, DKM, or DLKM issued by **JPK (Jabatan Pembangunan Kemahiran)** under the Ministry of Human Resources.

### 6. Continuous Improvement
We stay with the organisation after the certificate is issued — refining the training system, updating matrices, tracking retention, and planning the next certification wave. Skills development is a system, not an event.
