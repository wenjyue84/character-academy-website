# 02 — History & Founder

## Origin story

Character International Academy Sdn. Bhd. was founded by an industry practitioner — **Jennifer Tan** — who had spent decades running multi-sector businesses and saw the same problem in every one of them: organisations were relying on the memory and intuition of long-serving staff instead of on documented, teachable systems. The result was inconsistent performance, slow onboarding, and talent that walked out the door with the company's know-how still in their heads.

Character Academy was set up to fix that — to bring structured, competency-based training into Malaysian workplaces and to tie it to a nationally recognised certificate so that skilled work became a career path, not a guess.

## How the work evolved

In its early years, the academy worked directly with a small circle of industry partners to pilot training pathways, workplace integration, and competency assessments. As those pilots produced measurable results — more capable staff, better retention, formal certification — the model extended across sectors and across Johor, Negeri Sembilan, and beyond.

Today, the academy continues to uphold that founding purpose: pairing national standards (NOSS, SKM / DKM / DLKM) with real workplace training, so that Malaysian skilled workers earn recognition that matches their actual competence.

## Why it matters

The Malaysian government has placed TVET at the centre of its human-capital strategy under the **Twelfth Malaysia Plan** and the **National TVET Council (MTVET)** established December 2020. Budget 2025–2026 allocated **RM7.9 billion** to strengthen the TVET ecosystem. Character Academy's two-decade track record — **4,500+ learners certified, 80+ corporate partners** — is directly aligned with that national direction.
